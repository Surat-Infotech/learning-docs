# QA Career Paths and Certifications

You now have the core skills of a manual QA tester. So where does this lead? QA is
not a dead-end "entry job" — it's the start of several genuine career tracks, some
technical, some leadership-focused. This lesson maps the paths ahead and explains the
one certification you'll hear about most: **ISTQB**.

## What You'll Learn

- The common QA career tracks
- Where manual QA can take you
- What ISTQB is and whether it's worth it
- How to keep growing

## QA Is a Real Career, Not a Stepping Stone

A myth says QA is just a doorway to "real" development. Not true. Quality is a
discipline with senior, well-paid, respected roles. You *can* move into development
if you want — but you can also build a whole career in quality.

## The Main Career Tracks

```text
                        Junior QA / Manual Tester  (you start here)
                                    │
            ┌───────────────────────┼───────────────────────┐
            ▼                       ▼                       ▼
   AUTOMATION TRACK          SPECIALIST TRACK         LEADERSHIP TRACK
   QA Automation Engineer    Performance Tester       QA Lead
   SDET (test + code)        Security Tester          QA Manager
            │                Accessibility Tester     Head of Quality
            ▼                Mobile Specialist        Director of QA
   Test Architect                                     │
                                                      ▼
                                              (people + process focus)
```

### 1. Automation Track

Add coding to your testing skills and automate tests.

- **QA Automation Engineer** — writes automated tests (Selenium, Playwright,
  Cypress).
- **SDET (Software Development Engineer in Test)** — a hybrid who builds test
  frameworks and tools; strong coding required. Often the highest-paid IC track.
- **Test Architect** — designs the overall testing strategy and infrastructure.
- *Path:* manual QA → [API testing](../05-tools-and-process/03-api-testing-basics.md)
  → [a programming language](../../javascript-course/) → an
  [automation tool](../05-tools-and-process/05-intro-to-automation.md).

### 2. Specialist Track

Go deep in one demanding area:

- **Performance Tester** — load/stress testing with tools like JMeter, k6.
- **Security Tester** — finds vulnerabilities (often becomes penetration testing).
- **Accessibility Tester** — ensures apps work for people with disabilities.
- **Mobile QA Specialist** — deep expertise in mobile/device testing.

These specialties are in high demand and pay well because few people master them.

### 3. Leadership Track

Grow into managing people and process:

- **QA Lead** — guides a small team, owns test strategy for a project.
- **QA Manager** — manages testers, hiring, and process across teams.
- **Head of Quality / Director of QA** — sets quality strategy for the whole
  organization.

If you enjoy the *process and people* side (test plans, mentoring, improving how the
team works), this track suits you.

## The ISTQB Certification

**ISTQB (International Software Testing Qualifications Board)** is the most widely
recognized testing certification. The entry level is **ISTQB Foundation Level (CTFL)**.

- **What it covers:** exactly the fundamentals in *this course* — the seven
  principles, the STLC, test design techniques, defect management, test types, and
  the vocabulary. (If you've done this course, you're well on your way to the
  syllabus.)
- **Format:** a multiple-choice exam you can study for and take independently.
- **Why people get it:** it's recognized worldwide, some job listings ask for it, and
  it standardizes your vocabulary so you "speak QA" fluently.

**Is it worth it?** It depends:

- ✅ Helpful if you want a credential on your CV, are in a region/industry that
  values it, or want structured proof of fundamentals.
- ⚠️ Not a substitute for *doing the work* — a portfolio of real testing often
  impresses more than a certificate alone.
- 👍 Best combination: **solid skills + a portfolio + (optionally) ISTQB Foundation.**

This course maps closely to the ISTQB Foundation syllabus, so if you want the cert,
you've already done much of the learning — you'd mainly review the official syllabus
and practice exam questions.

## How to Keep Growing

- **Test more, deliberately.** Every app you use is practice.
- **Read** testing blogs and communities (Ministry of Testing is a popular one).
- **Learn API testing**, then dip into **automation** if it appeals.
- **Find a niche** (mobile, performance, security, accessibility) if you want to
  specialize.
- **Build in public** — keep adding to your [portfolio](01-building-a-qa-portfolio.md).
